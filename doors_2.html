01

Floors

The five main play areas of DOORS — each with unique rooms, hazards, entities, and atmosphere

DOORS is divided into distinct floors. Each floor escalates in danger, introduces new entity types, and features unique room generation. Completing a floor's final door earns an achievement and advances the game. Dying resets you to the elevator — all progress in the current run is lost (unless using a Lotus/Starfish revival item).

🏨
Floor 1 · Available
The Hotel

The original and iconic starting floor. A crumbling Victorian hotel spanning 100 numbered doors. Split into sections: The Reception (doors 0–10), The Grand Hallway, The Library (door 50 — mandatory Figure encounter), The Dining Hall, The Courtyard (door ~89), The Greenhouse rooms (doors ~90–99), and The Electrical Room (door 100 — final boss encounter).

Doors 0–100 Elevator entry Rush · Figure · Seek · Screech · Eyes · Halt ~45–90 min Max 6 players
🌑
Sub-Floor · Infinite Loop
The Rooms

An infinite, looping sub-floor accessible via a hidden exit at approximately door 60 in The Hotel. Yellow-tiled, liminal corridors with no end. Houses A-60, A-90, and A-120. Players loop endlessly — the goal for the "A-1000" achievement requires surviving 1,000 rooms. A red barrier prevents entry while modifiers are active.

Secret access ~door 60 A-60 · A-90 · A-120 Infinite loop A-1000 goal
🔮
Sub-Floor · Post-Hunt
The Backdoor

A dimension accessible after The Hunt: First Edition event. Separate from The Rooms, this dimension introduced Haste — an entity that attacks if a Backdoor timer runs out. Levers must be activated to reset the timer. Players can now reach The Backdoor using the "Other Way Around" modifier which was added post-Hunt. Features looping corridors with unique pacing challenges.

Requires modifier unlock Haste · A-60 · A-120 Lever timer mechanic Other Way Around badge req.
⛏️
Floor 2 · Added August 2024
The Mines

A sprawling underground mining complex beneath the Hotel. Spans 200+ doors across several distinct sub-sections: The Adit, The Shafts, The Caverns (with Grumble nests), The Nest (door 150 — Grumble boss), The Sewers, The Dam (door 200 — Seek encounter with valve puzzle), and The Woods exit. New hazards include Firedamp gas, Floods, and Minecarts.

Doors 1–200+ Grumble · Dread · Figure (Mines) · Seek (Dam) New hazards: Firedamp, Floods, Minecarts ~90–180 min
🌿
Floor 3 · Added August 2025
The Outdoors

An overgrown, post-apocalyptic outdoor environment accessible from The Woods at the end of The Mines. Introduced The Groundskeeper as a major new entity. Features unique environmental hazards and organic room layouts. The "Down The Water Spout" achievement is tied to completing this floor. Enables new modifier options when unlocked.

Floor 3 — Outdoors Groundskeeper · Snare New mechanics Sodded modifier unlocked

02

Sublevels & Special Rooms

Mandatory set-pieces, hidden chambers, and pivotal rooms within each floor

Within each floor are special rooms that break the normal numbered-door sequence. Some are mandatory boss encounters with their own mechanics; others are optional secrets or random side rooms. Knowing what to expect prevents cheap deaths.

🚗 The Reception (Door 0 — Hotel)

The starting point. Players spawn in a lobby area with a short hallway introducing basic controls. Features El Goblino and Guiding Light for tutorial context. No hostile entities spawn here, but lootable furniture is present.

  • No entity threat — exploration only
  • Guiding Light provides gameplay tips
  • El Goblino gives hints and dialogue
  • First Jeff Shop appears around doors 5–10

📚 The Library (Door 50 — Hotel)

The first mandatory boss encounter in the game. A massive library completely patrolled by Figure. Players must collect all scattered books while moving silently. Figure navigates entirely by sound — crouch at all times, never sprint, avoid knocking items.

  • Crouch-walk only — never sprint or jump
  • Books must all be collected to progress
  • If grabbed: survive a heartbeat QTE minigame
  • Inhibitor item greatly reduces Figure's hearing range
  • Closets are safe — Hide entity timer still applies

⚡ The Electrical Room (Door 100 — Hotel)

The climactic final encounter of Floor 1. Players must restart generators/breakers across the room while Figure patrols. Once all power is restored, the final elevator opens. Figure is faster and more aggressive here than in the Library.

  • Generator puzzle — activate all nodes
  • Figure immediately pursues on loud noise
  • Completing it earns "Rock Bottom" achievement
  • Vitamins recommended before entering
  • Closets available but Hide timer is tight

🌿 The Courtyard (Door ~89 — Hotel)

An outdoor-adjacent greenhouse-style room containing Snare entities embedded in the floor. The Shears item found here is used to cut vines on a locked vine door, opening a secret path. Required for the "Detour" achievement.

  • Snare entities hide in floor patches
  • Shears found on a table — used on vine door
  • Skeleton Key unlocks the door behind the vines
  • "Detour" achievement for taking the vine path

🕳️ The Rooms Entrance (Door ~60 — Hotel)

A hidden door disguised among normal numbered doors, found approximately around door 60 in the Hotel. It opens to the infinite yellow-tiled corridors of The Rooms. The entrance is subtle — only identifiable by its plain unmarked appearance. Entry is blocked when modifiers are active.

  • Random spawn window around doors 55–65
  • Blocked entirely when any modifier is enabled
  • A-60, A-90, A-120 inhabit The Rooms
  • "Back On Track" badge for reaching A-1000

🛗 Jeff's Shop (Random — All Floors)

The friendly shopkeeper Jeff appears in a warmly-lit room identifiable by the soft glow under the door and quiet humming. Players spend Gold found in drawers and shelves to buy items. Jeff is completely harmless. A Donation Jar in his shop enables the "Pls Donate" achievement if filled.

  • Appears randomly throughout all floors
  • Stock changes each visit; some items floor-specific
  • Gold is the in-shop currency (different from Knobs)
  • Tipping the jar fully unlocks "Pls Donate"

🪲 The Nest (Door 150 — Mines)

A mandatory sub-section in The Mines at door 150. Players must activate all anchor points inside the Nest while Grumble and Queen Grumble patrol the area. Completing it earns "Trespassing" and unlocks the path to The Sewers.

  • Activate all anchors to progress
  • Grumble and Queen Grumble patrol actively
  • Crucifix can banish regular Grumbles (not Queen)
  • "Trespassing" achievement on escape

🌊 The Dam (Door 200 — Mines)

The climactic final encounter of Floor 2. A flooded dam area where players must rotate all valves to lower the water level, while Seek (in Mines variant form) actively pursues them through the flooding chamber. One of the most intense sequences in the game.

  • Rotate all valves to drain the dam
  • Seek is present and chasing throughout
  • Floods slow movement dramatically
  • "Down The Drain" achievement on success
  • "See You Soon" earned for completing Floor 2

🎪 Rift (Random — Hotel/Mines)

A rare, swirling purple portal that occasionally appears in rooms. Walking through it teleports players to a brief alternate space containing bonus loot, unique Candle items, or the Possessed Candle. Required for the "Interconnected" achievement. Cannot be used when certain modifiers are active.

  • Rare random spawn — not guaranteed each run
  • Contains bonus items and Gold
  • Possessed Candle found here → "Lost In Translation" badge
  • "Interconnected" badge for entering a Rift
  • Disabled by specific modifiers (No Rift tag)

Entities

Every creature, monster, helper, and force inhabiting the floors — with accurate counters

Entities are DOORS' central threat mechanic. Each has a unique detection system, attack pattern, warning sign, and counter method. Health does not regenerate — every hit stacks, and dying resets the entire run. Learn the warning signs before the entity, not during.

Items

Everything findable, buyable, and usable across all floors

Items are found in shelves, drawers, and chests — or purchased at Jeff's shop with Gold. Each has limited uses. Inventory space is finite. Prioritize healing and light sources above everything else, then utility items depending on your run's needs.

Modifiers

All 80 run-altering modifiers — with their exact effects and Knob bonuses

Modifiers are unlocked by completing The Hotel without dying (enabling the Modifiers menu). Additional modifiers unlock via specific badges. Negative modifiers add bonus Knob multipliers on run completion. The maximum bonus is +150% before it becomes halved; the minimum is capped at −90%. There are currently 80 modifiers (including Admin Panel). Completing The Hotel with +50% bonus earns "Not Five Stars"; +150% earns "Hotel Hell." The Mines equivalents are "Rocky Road" (+50%) and "A Hard Place" (+150%).

🔓 How to Unlock The Modifiers menu is unlocked by completing The Hotel without dying at least once. Individual modifiers unlock through specific badges. Some require "Not Five Stars," "Hotel Hell," or other achievement prerequisites. Random modifiers (Uh Oh, How Unfortunate, Chaos Chaos) can select from any unlocked modifier pool.

Rarities

Item drop tiers — what they mean and what to look for

◆ Common

The baseline drop tier. Found constantly in drawers throughout the run. Pick up early but don't fill inventory with only these.

  • Bandage — 20 HP heal
  • Lighter — basic unlimited light
  • Small Gold amounts (1–10)
  • Flashlight — basic directional light
  • Standard Keys

◆ Uncommon

Appears every 5–15 rooms on average. Meaningful utility — generally worth keeping over commons when inventory space is tight.

  • Vitamins — full HP + speed
  • Candle — area ambient light
  • Shears — vine door only
  • Mid Gold clusters (10–30)
  • Lockpick — bypasses locks

◆ Rare

Genuinely exciting to find in a drawer. These items can shift the difficulty curve of an entire section significantly.

  • Adrenaline — sprint boost
  • Skeleton Key — opens any door
  • Possessed Candle — Rift only
  • Gold Key — premium lock
  • Inhibitor — reduces Figure hearing

◆ Epic

High-impact items rarely found through normal looting. Primarily obtainable from Jeff's shop. Can single-handedly save a run from failure.

  • Crucifix — banish entities
  • Lotus / Starfish — revive on death
  • Herb of Viridis — Jeff secret unlock
  • Large Gold clusters (30–60)

◆ Legendary

Extraordinarily rare — finding one in a drawer is a genuine surprise. These drastically alter a run's outcome.

  • Very large Gold deposits (60+)
  • Rare Crucifix variants
  • Special lore documents
  • Unique event-adjacent items

◆ Event

Only obtainable during active seasonal events. Cannot be found or purchased outside the event window. Often cosmetic or novelty.

  • Trick Or Treat Candy items
  • Holiday-themed cosmetic drops
  • Event-exclusive loot

Survival Guide

Essential strategies, hidden mechanics, and expert-level tactics

These tips assume you've at least seen each entity once. If you're completely new — just play your first few runs blind for the authentic experience. Then come back here.

01

The flicker is your alarm. Rush causes lights to flicker 2–4 seconds before entering. The moment you see ANY flickering — sprint to a wardrobe or bed and get inside. Don't wait to confirm it's Rush. React first, confirm second.

02

Ambush wants you impatient. After a Rush/Ambush sweep, wait for the audio cue (a distinct 'despawn' sound) to fully stop. Ambush never fully despawns unless crucified — it WILL come back. Rushing out early is why most people die to it.

03

Figure is a sound god. In the Library and Electrical Room: crouch constantly, never sprint, never jump. Walking near shelves you can bump into knocks books. Open drawers only when Figure is on the far side of the room. The Inhibitor item cuts its hearing range significantly.

04

Screech says "pst" exactly once. Turn on a light source in every dark room immediately. If you hear "pst," scan the room fast and look DIRECTLY at Screech within ~3 seconds. If you're already looking at a flashlight's beam path, that usually covers it.

05

Eyes is about camera direction, not sight. When Eyes appears, stare at the floor, wall, or ceiling. Even having Eyes in the edge of your peripheral view deals damage. The goal is zero eye contact — not just "not staring at it."

06

Seek's walls tell you everything. Eyes appearing on walls and floors = Seek is 15–30 seconds away. Use this time to mentally note obstacles in the upcoming corridor. Equip Adrenaline before the chase triggers, not during.

07

A-90 demands total paralysis. When A-90 appears, release every key and stop all mouse movement. Do NOT try to hide, sprint, or crouch. Literally nothing — not even a millimeter of mouse drift — is the counter. Wait 3–5 seconds for it to vanish.

08

Halt is a rhythm game. When Halt appears, walk forward (toward it) while facing it. As it nearly reaches you, turn 180° immediately. The hallway resets. Repeat until the hallway section ends. Never run away from it — walk toward it, then turn.

09

Dupe doors are one digit off. If the door says the same number as the room you're in, or the previous room's number — it's Dupe. If it says the number that should come NEXT in sequence — it's real. When in doubt, check the key in your hand (if you have one) — it shows the real door number.

10

The Lotus / Starfish is run insurance. At 200 Gold from Jeff, it's the best purchase in the game. You lose absolutely nothing by buying it early. Think of it as paying 200 Gold to protect a 60–90 minute investment. Buy it before Vitamins if you're choosing.

11

Gold and Knobs are different currencies. Gold is found in drawers and shelves — spent at Jeff's shop in-run. Knobs are the lobby currency earned on run completion/death — spent in the pre-run shop. Never confuse them. Modifiers multiply your Knob earnings at run end, not your Gold mid-run.

12

The Rooms block modifiers — plan ahead. You cannot enter The Rooms if any modifier is active (blocked by a red barrier). If you want both the modifier bonus AND a Rooms run, you need separate runs. The Backdoor (via Other Way Around modifier) is a different sub-floor and IS accessible with modifiers.

Lore & Story

The narrative buried in environments, journals, entity behavior, and developer hints

DOORS communicates its story through environmental details, hidden lore documents, entity behavior, and developer statements. Multiple playthroughs and careful observation are needed to piece together the full picture. The following reflects confirmed or strongly implied lore.

The Three Architects

Developer hints and in-game paintings near The Rooms entrance suggest a trio of supernatural entities — "The Three Architects" — who govern the hotel's reality. Guiding Light (golden, helpful), a neutral figure (possibly tied to modifiers and the Red Architect motif), and a dark third presence. The spacetime painting near The Rooms entrance is cited as evidence of their roles. Modifiers themselves may be lore-relevant to these figures.

  • Guiding Light = helpful gold architect
  • Red Architect = modifier-related, controlling difficulty
  • Third architect — nature unclear
  • Modifier symbols appear next to Crucifix paintings

Figure's Origin

Figure is heavily implied to have once been human — or at minimum, was created by a human event rather than a supernatural one. Lore documents in the Hotel reference a catastrophic incident that cleared the building of its staff and guests. Figure appears to be something that resulted from or was drawn in by that event. Its behavior (patrolling, reacting to sound, seeming almost habitual) suggests remnant human instincts.

  • Blind — relies entirely on sound
  • Patrols the Library as if on routine
  • Entities were canonically named by "the person the player represents" — not their real names
  • Lore docs reference a pre-closure Hotel incident

Seek's Nature

Seek is described in old Hotel documents as "The Watchful Dark" — a collective presence that observes before it acts. The eyes that appear on walls before Seek's chase sequence are confirmed to be extensions of Seek itself, not separate entities. Seek grows larger and more aggressive in its second Hotel encounter, suggesting it feeds on or grows from the player's presence. In The Mines, Seek manifests differently — through the flooding Dam sequence — suggesting it adapts to its environment.

  • The wall/floor eyes ARE Seek — pre-attack observation
  • Grows more powerful per encounter in same run
  • Adapts to the environment (Hotel halls → Dam flooding)
  • Crucifix can interrupt a chase for "Take A Breather"

The Rooms & The Backdoor

The Rooms are an infinite liminal space — a yellow-tiled, looping non-place. Developer statements confirm it connects to a separate game's universe, which is why A-60, A-90, and A-120 exist there. The Backdoor is a related but distinct pocket dimension introduced after The Hunt, featuring its own entity Haste and lever-based timer mechanic. Both spaces represent "leaks" or boundaries between the Hotel's reality and something adjacent to it.

  • The Rooms = cross-universe crossover dimension
  • A-60, A-90, A-120 originate from the connected game universe
  • The Backdoor = distinct from The Rooms (post-Hunt addition)
  • Red barrier = modifiers prevent entry to The Rooms (lore-relevant)

The Mines' Purpose

The Mines beneath the Hotel were not originally a standard mining operation. Research areas deep in The Mines suggest they predate the Hotel incident and were conducting experimental work of some kind. The presence of Dread, Grumble colonies, and unique entity behavior in the Mines suggests the underground environment has its own distinct ecosystem of dangers — separate from the Hotel's entities — that pre-existed human involvement.

  • Mines sub-sections: Adit → Shafts → Caverns → Nest → Sewers → Dam → Woods
  • Firedamp and Flood hazards suggest abandoned industrial/research use
  • Grumbles inhabit The Nest as their home territory
  • Figure also appears in the Mines — suggesting it followed players down

The Outdoors

The Outdoors is the most recent floor (Aug 2025) and represents the world beyond the Hotel and Mines — an overgrown, post-abandonment outdoor environment. The Groundskeeper is the floor's primary entity, patrolling outdoor paths as if maintaining the property. The implication is that whatever event caused the Hotel's closure also affected the surrounding grounds, leaving them dangerous and overgrown with the Groundskeeper as the only "maintenance" presence.

  • First outdoor floor — no enclosed rooms
  • Groundskeeper patrols outdoor landmark routes
  • Snare entities present throughout
  • Completing it earns "Touched Grass" and "Down The Water Spout"
